INCIDENT RESPONSE POLICY
Version: 3.0 | Effective Date: February 1, 2025

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes procedures for detecting, reporting, assessing, responding to,
and recovering from information security incidents to minimize business impact.

2. SCOPE
Applies to all employees, contractors, and vendors who operate or support company systems.

3. INCIDENT CLASSIFICATION
3.1 SEVERITY 1 (CRITICAL): Data breach, ransomware, critical system compromise. Response: Immediate.
3.2 SEVERITY 2 (HIGH): Unauthorized access to sensitive systems, malware detection. Response: Within 1 hour.
3.3 SEVERITY 3 (MEDIUM): Phishing attacks, policy violations, failed intrusion attempts. Response: Within 4 hours.
3.4 SEVERITY 4 (LOW): Minor security events, informational alerts. Response: Within 24 hours.

4. DETECTION & REPORTING
4.1 All employees must report suspected security incidents to security@company.com immediately.
4.2 Security monitoring tools must be deployed to detect anomalous activity 24/7.
4.3 Failure to report a known incident within the required timeframe is a HIGH-RISK violation.
4.4 Anonymous reporting channels must be available and protected from retaliation.

5. RESPONSE PROCEDURES
5.1 CONTAINMENT: Isolate affected systems to prevent spread within 30 minutes of detection.
5.2 ERADICATION: Remove threat actors, malware, or vulnerabilities from all affected systems.
5.3 RECOVERY: Restore systems from verified clean backups only after eradication is confirmed.
5.4 System restoration without eradication confirmation is a HIGH-RISK compliance violation.

6. PATCH MANAGEMENT
6.1 Critical security patches must be applied within 72 hours of vendor release.
6.2 High-severity patches must be applied within 30 days.
6.3 Systems running unpatched critical vulnerabilities are classified as HIGH-RISK.
6.4 Exceptions require written CISO approval and must include a compensating control plan.
6.5 Monthly vulnerability scans are mandatory across all internet-facing systems.

7. BACKUP & RECOVERY
7.1 Critical systems must be backed up daily with a Recovery Time Objective (RTO) of 4 hours.
7.2 Backup integrity must be tested monthly through restoration drills.
7.3 Backup media must be stored in a geographically separate location from primary systems.
7.4 Systems without tested backups in the last 90 days are a MEDIUM compliance risk.

8. POST-INCIDENT REVIEW
8.1 A Post-Incident Report (PIR) must be completed within 5 business days of incident resolution.
8.2 Root cause analysis must identify contributing factors and preventive measures.
8.3 Lessons learned must be shared with relevant teams and control owners.

9. REGULATORY NOTIFICATION
9.1 Data breaches affecting personal data must be reported to regulators within 72 hours (GDPR).
9.2 Legal counsel must be engaged for all Severity 1 and Severity 2 incidents.
